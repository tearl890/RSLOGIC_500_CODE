Since Project 1 is a basic start/stop-with-memory style PLC exercise (pump runs until a high-pressure condition is reached, then stays off until pressure drops below the low-pressure threshold), here are some similar-level practice projects:

### Practice Project 2 – Water Tank Level Control

**Process**
Maintain water level in a tank using one fill pump.

**Inputs**

* I:0/0 – Low level switch (ON when water is above low level)
* I:0/1 – High level switch (ON when water reaches full level)

**Outputs**

* O:0/0 – Fill pump
* O:0/1 – Tank Full light

**Test Criteria**

1. Both switches OFF → Pump ON, Light OFF.
2. Low level switch ON → Pump stays ON, Light OFF.
3. Both switches ON → Pump OFF, Light ON.
4. High level OFF while low level remains ON → Pump remains OFF.
5. Both switches OFF → Pump ON again.

---

### Practice Project 3 – Cooling Fan Control

**Process**
Control temperature using a cooling fan.

**Inputs**

* I:0/0 – Temperature above 70°F
* I:0/1 – Temperature above 90°F

**Outputs**

* O:0/0 – Cooling fan
* O:0/1 – High temperature light

**Test Criteria**

1. Both inputs OFF → Fan OFF, Light OFF.
2. 70°F switch ON → Fan ON.
3. Both switches ON → Fan remains ON, Light ON.
4. 90°F switch OFF but 70°F switch ON → Fan stays ON.
5. Both OFF → Fan OFF.

---

### Practice Project 4 – Conveyor Product Accumulation

**Process**
Run a conveyor until a storage area is full.

**Inputs**

* I:0/0 – Storage area occupied
* I:0/1 – Storage area full

**Outputs**

* O:0/0 – Conveyor motor
* O:0/1 – Full indicator

**Test Criteria**

1. No sensors ON → Conveyor ON.
2. Occupied sensor ON → Conveyor remains ON.
3. Full sensor ON → Conveyor OFF, indicator ON.
4. Full sensor OFF while occupied remains ON → Conveyor stays OFF.
5. Both sensors OFF → Conveyor ON.

---

### Practice Project 5 – Air Compressor with Alarm

**Process**
Same concept as the original project, but add an alarm.

**Inputs**

* I:0/0 – Low pressure switch (90 psi)
* I:0/1 – High pressure switch (110 psi)

**Outputs**

* O:0/0 – Compressor
* O:0/1 – Pressure OK light
* O:0/2 – High pressure alarm

**Requirements**

* Compressor operation identical to Project 1.
* Pressure OK light ON whenever low pressure switch is ON.
* Alarm ON whenever high pressure switch is ON.

---

### Practice Project 6 – Two-Pump Lead Control

**Process**
Maintain level using two pumps.

**Inputs**

* I:0/0 – Low level switch
* I:0/1 – High level switch

**Outputs**

* O:0/0 – Pump 1
* O:0/1 – Pump 2

**Requirements**

* When level is below low switch, both pumps run.
* When high switch turns ON, both pumps stop.
* Pumps remain stopped until level drops below low switch.

---

### Challenge Version (Slightly Harder)

**Inputs**

* I:0/0 – Low pressure switch
* I:0/1 – High pressure switch
* I:0/2 – Auto/Manual selector

**Outputs**

* O:0/0 – Pump
* O:0/1 – Pressure light

**Requirements**

* In Auto, behave exactly like Project 1.
* In Manual, pump runs continuously regardless of pressure switches.
* Pressure light still follows pressure conditions.

Try solving these without looking at the answer first. Start with a state table showing all possible input combinations and desired output states, then write the ladder logic. That's exactly the thought process you'll use later for PLC interviews and real troubleshooting.
